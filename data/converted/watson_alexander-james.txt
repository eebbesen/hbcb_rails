   Retired

  presented with gold long service medal in honour of his 30 years with the Company on 28 Oct. 1939; newspaper clipping on file records the following

   career summary: "Mr. Watson was born in Yorkshire, England, and at the age of 12 went to South Africa. Three years later he returned to England,

   where he served his apprenticeship in the drygoods business [with Marshall & Snelgrove]. In 1903 he came to Canada, first settling in Manitoba and later

   coming to British Columbia, where he engaged in fruit ranching [near Nelson]. In September, 1909, he joined the Hudson's Bay Company in Vancouver

   as a salesman and soon advanced to buyer and merchandise manager there" (Victoria Daily Times, 28 Oct. 1938, p. 11[item on file in RG2/38/122]).

  received letter from the Mayor of Victoria conveying thanks and appreciation of the city for his co-operation and assistance rendered to civic authorities

   during his business career with the Company in Victoria (dated 11 Jan. 1944; see RG2/38/122)

  presented with inscribed silver bowl at dinner held in his honour on 24 Jan. 1944, commemorating 35 years of service with the HBC

  wife: Maude Watson, d. 4 Jan. 1958
