The following children were baptized at Red River:

John (1836), James (1838), Margaret (1840), Maria (1842), William (1844), Philip (1846),

Nancy (1848), Thomas (1849), Joseph (1853) and Magnus (1855).

The baptism of their youngest child, Henry, is recorded in the Prince Albert and Nepowewan

Anglican Parish Register on 3 April 1859.

In the 1881 census for Prince Albert, Margaret Bear is recorded as a widow, living with her son Joseph

on the John Smith Reserve.

In the 1843 Red River Census, William Bear is recorded as residing in the Swampy Village, a native of

Rupert's Land and Protestant. He was married, with one son under 16, one daughter above 15, and

two daughters under 15.*

He owned a house and stable, three oxen, three cows, two calves, three pigs, a harrow and a canoe.                  E.5/11, fo. 31d.-32

*The format in which information on children was recorded made mistakes quite likely.
