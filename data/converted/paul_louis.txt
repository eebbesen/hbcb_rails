*An Outfit year ran from 1 June to 31 May

Louis Paul, a native of Rupert's Land, signed a contract with the Hudson's Bay Company on 2 May, 1853 to serve as a middleman for 3 years as

from 1 June, 1853 at wages of 20 pounds sterling per annum, in the Saskatchewan District. In 1858 he became a bowsman and remained in that

capacity until 1860 when he left the service of the Company to become a freeman in the Saskatchewan District.

(HBC Archives A.32/49,fo.140; B.239/g/38,fo.27d; B.239/g/40,fo.61)
