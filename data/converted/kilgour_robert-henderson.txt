Born in Leith, Scotland. Joined H.B.C. as an apprentice in 1929, being appointed to the Western Arctic District.  He was at Aklavik in 1931, and

later had charge of Baillie Island and Holman Island posts. He was appointed to Lake Harbour, Baffin Island, in   1941, where he was drowned in

1943.
