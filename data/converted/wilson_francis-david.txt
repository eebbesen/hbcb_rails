According to his own accounts of his career (A.12/FT 334/1c, fo.127) Wilson had five years of business training before joining the HBC. He

mentions serving only at Edmonton and Fort Vermilion before going to James Bay, yet according to one record he was in charge of Fort Victoria

(Alberta), 1889-1890 (A.12 FT 334/1c, fo.130) and according to another, in charge of Fort Chipewyan, 1895-1898 (A.12/FT 3240/5 (Misc.), fo.38)

After his retirement, he purchased items for the HBC Museum Collection. The Beaver, June/July 1994, p.14

He was married with 3 children, one of whom was killed on active service. A.12/FT 334/1c, fo.130

In recommending his promotion to District Manager, Fur Trade Commissioner, R.H. Hall wrote:

Mr. Wilson is an experienced officer of ability and strong personality. He has also shown that he can control those under him and hold their

respect....He is not only a good accountant and capable man of affairs, but his mechanical genius will be of great service in respect to repairs or

construction of steamers or other vessels...                                                                 A.12/FT 340/5(Misc.) , fo.36
