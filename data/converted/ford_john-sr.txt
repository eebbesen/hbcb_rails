1869, 23 April           entered into a contract with Donald Smith to take from the HBC                    A.32/29 fo. 93-94

                         the fishing post at Paul's Island for two years. He was to provide

                         crew, craft and gear, with the HBC providing 2/3 of nets and lines.

                         All the fish were to go to HBC, with the Company paying regular

                         prices at Davis Inlet for 2/3, and the other 1/3 going to the HBC.

                         The HBC was also to provide traps for 1/4 of the furs caught, and

                         get paid for the rest. He was also to be allowed ordinary prices

                         for cod and trout and 7 shillings per pair for "best Esquimaux boots".
